Beliefs about Teaching & Learning

As I got ready for my upcoming deployment, I felt excitement and anticipation. I distinctly recalled my time as a student, watching student teachers at our school. They seemed to handle their responsibilities effortlessly, and I often thought their jobs looked simple.

In my mind, teaching involved tasks that appeared clear and uncomplicated. I thought I would grasp educational material well enough that memorizing lessons would happen naturally. I envisioned carefully preparing PowerPoint presentations, assigning homework, and coming up with fun activities to keep students engaged.

I believed that teaching was mostly about sharing knowledge from teacher to student. I was eager to put into practice the skills and theories I had learned during my studies in a real classroom. It was not just about teaching — it was also a chance to show my readiness and passion for positively impacting students' education.

After observing during the first semester, I quickly realized that teaching is much more complicated and layered than I had originally thought. I learned that being a teacher goes beyond just presenting lessons and grading assignments. Through my experiences with my cooperating teacher, I saw that teaching requires flexibility, emotional understanding, and ongoing problem-solving skills.

What struck me most was how a teacher has to take on many roles, often at the same time. Teachers aren't just people who know the subject; they also serve as caregivers, mentors, and emotional support for their students.

One of the biggest insights I gained was that teaching means being like a second parent to the students. The duty of guiding, caring for, and supporting students extends beyond the classroom. I've come to realize that teaching is more than just a job — it is a meaningful calling that requires a strong dedication to influencing the lives of young people.

Teaching is about forming connections with students and creating a welcoming and safe environment where they can learn and grow. The influence a teacher has on their students can last a lifetime. Teaching is not only about the information you share; it's about the love, care, and commitment you provide.

Reflection

After experiencing different aspects of teaching, I've learned that being a teacher is both challenging and rewarding. It's a demanding job that requires a lot of patience and effort. Teachers' responsibilities extend beyond the classroom — often spilling into personal time as they spend hours planning lessons, grading work, and meeting deadlines.

What makes teaching satisfying is the chance to make a real difference in students' lives. It goes beyond just sharing knowledge; it's about sparking curiosity, helping students reach their potential, and building connections. The happiness of seeing students understand a topic or simply feeling supported makes all the effort worthwhile.

Being a teacher also involves continuous learning. Interacting with students offers valuable lessons in understanding others, bouncing back from setbacks, and thinking creatively. These shared moments create a special connection between teachers and students.

This awareness drives me to pursue excellence, knowing my work helps shape future generations. The saying "lagi't lagi para sa bata at sa bayan" (always for the child and the nation) truly connects with me. It shows that teaching is not just a job but a promise to support a bigger cause — nurturing growth, providing strength, and inspiring hope for a brighter future.
